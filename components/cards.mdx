# Cards are quick choices

Cards help you present a small set of clear options or highlights. In Explorer Elite, they are useful when readers should compare paths, spot features, or jump to the next page quickly.

## Good card examples

<Cards>
  <Card title="Choose your path (Explorer / Partner)" icon="compass" horizontal>
    Use this when the reader needs to choose a direction quickly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Feature highlights" icon="sparkles" horizontal>
    Use this when you want to show the most important benefits at a glance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Next steps links" icon="arrow-right" horizontal>
    Use this when the page should guide people to another useful place.
  </Card>
</Cards>

## Keep cards easy to scan

- Keep titles short.
- Use one-sentence summaries.
- Show only the most useful choices.
- Keep the wording simple enough to scan in a few seconds.

<Callout kind="info">
Cards work best when the reader is scanning for a path, not reading a long explanation.
</Callout>
